Carbon Carmen

Weight 26 lbs Thrusters 6x Bilge Pumps
Dimensions 25.0" x 21.0" x 6.0" Cameras 3x SS-AquaCam
Processor/Controller ???

Carbon Carmen is the team’s 2011-2012 underwater robot, a remotely operated vehicle (ROV) for the MATE competition. The name originates from the unique use of carbon fiber for the electronics housing and Ohio State’s traditional post-football song, Carmen Ohio.

This vehicle was the team’s first attempt at creating a watertight enclosure for an integrated electronics system. This vehicle is designed for speedy movement on the horizontal plane and easy access to internal electronic components. The carbon fiber electronics housing is made of two identical rectangular sections, sealed in between with an ultra-soft gasket. Wires coming in and out of the ROV passed through custom made sealing blocks. Propulsion was achieved with 6 thrusters, four facing forward and two positioned vertically. A cage-like protection and mounting system was designed to hold cameras and actuators and shield the electronics housing. The electronics system consisted of a custom made PCB with 6 individual motor controllers, which was then controlled with a LabVIEW program on a topside computer.

Features:
  • Custom Cable Sealing Blocks
  • Superior Mobility In The Horizontal Plane
  • Custom PCB - for what?
  • Commercial Waterproof Cameras
