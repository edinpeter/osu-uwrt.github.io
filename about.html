The Team

Founded in 2010, the Underwater Robotics Team (UWRT) is an emerging engineering project team at The Ohio State University. The team is comprised of a multidisciplinary group of undergraduate students interested in design, manufacturing, and engineering in the unique arena of an underwater environment. Specifically we are designing and building a Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) as well as an Autonomous Underwater Vechile (AUV). Our current team members are studying in the fields of mechanical engineering, aerospace engineering, electrical engineering, and computer science engineering but the team is open to students of all majors and skills.

Current Projects

Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV)

We are currently developing an ROV that will compete in the 2015 MATE Competition located in Labrador, Canada. This tethered underwater robot will be controlled from the surface by the team and used to complete a variety of tasks as required by the competition.

Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV)

We are also developing an AUV that will compete in the 2016 Robosub Competition located in San Diego, California. Currently in the design phase, our AUV is following in the footsteps of our ROV in both design and operation. The fundemental difference being the AUV will be designed to run and complete tasks independently. Our most ambitious project to-date, our team is excited to take on this challenge.
